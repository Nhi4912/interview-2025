# AGENTS.md — 09-behavioral

## OVERVIEW

Behavioral interview **delivery** toolkit: STAR framework, LP vocabulary mapping, common-question drill
set, and narrative storytelling craft. Distinct from `../08-l5-competencies/` — that folder is the
**scoring rubric** (what gets evaluated); this folder is **how to answer** (technique and practice).

## STUDY ORDER

1. `01-star-method.md` — foundation framework; internalize before anything else
2. `04-storytelling.md` — narrative craft layered on top of STAR (hooks, tension, callback, voice)
3. `02-leadership-principles.md` — company-specific LP vocabulary; map your stories to each company's
   framework (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Grab differ meaningfully)
4. `03-common-questions.md` — drill set with worked answers; use after you have stories ready

## ANTI-PATTERNS

- **Don't merge with `../08-l5-competencies/`** — rubric (what is scored) and delivery (how to
  answer) serve different purposes; keep them separate.
- **Don't treat `02-leadership-principles.md` as Amazon-only** — Microsoft, Grab, and Google have
  distinct value frameworks; document the mapping per company, don't paste Amazon LPs wholesale.
- **Don't pad `03-common-questions.md` with theory** — it's a drill set; keep answers tight and
  story-shaped, not essay-length explanations.
- **Don't strip the storytelling craft from `04-storytelling.md`** — hooks, tension arcs, and
  authentic voice are what separate strong candidates from adequate ones; preserve them.
- **Don't renumber files** — other documents and agents reference filenames by number; renaming
  breaks those links silently.
